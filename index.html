Who Am I?

Here’s a little PINCH about me: I’m just a lil’ lady residing in the city of otherly Love--that’d be Philadelphia, PA. Growing up I was a shy little shrimp to the outside world but at home I was singing into my jumbo size Crayola crayon, that was obviously my microphone, along side my mom’s VHS tape of Madonna’s Like a Virgin Tour for all you 80’s babies out there. My dad always had his camcorder running so I’ve got the evidence if you need more visual proof. I’d say performing was deep in there at an early age for sure. I also convinced my grandmother that Chris Columbus should consider making another Home Alone movie and cast me as Macaulay Culkin’s love interest--I didn’t have any feelings for Mr. Culkin I just thought I could act in a movie. By the time I finished high school I was a full-throttle ballerina and budding thespian. I loved to dance but I knew it wasn’t something I took seriously enough to pursue professionally. I still love to boogie and dancing will always be one of my favorite things to do. It was, however, the performance aspect that hooked me.

MOVING ON! Then college happened and if I’m being honest it didn’t suit me. I studied theatre and it seemed too curricular, too memorize all this information over load to spit it back out to only forget it years later at trivia night at the local bar. Don't get me wrong, I have full respect for Mr. Shakespeare and the history of theatre and performance but I was itching to be on my feet moving and exploring what boundaries theatre could push through. So in my haste to graduate I was introduced to The Pig Iron Theatre Company’s show “Mission to Mercury” and was blown away by what I saw. I never saw a performance like this and thought, “ I wanna make that kind of theatre.” After college was filled with acting classes, auditioning, working in the service industry as a waitress and bartender, to more acting classes, [ click on that resume link to see all that training woof! ] and to joining a traveling improv group. But still after all those amazing experiences I still felt lost. I was confused about what kind of actor I was.

ONWARD! After a few years of trying to pay my dues as a struggling artist my beloved Pig Iron Theatre Co. started a two year advanced training program rooted in Lecoq pedagogy and exploring the creation of theatre as an ensemble. My fourteen peers and I were trained movement, mask, character work ( gee whiz I love wearing wigs ) mime,cabaret, and clown. Yea....clown. And for anyone who’s got a bad case of coulrophobia--fear of clowns please skip to the end! I currently work with a non-profit called Healthy Humor Inc. as a healthcare clown at Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia. I try my hardest to make the kiddos in that hospital smile. It’s one of the most rewarding jobs I’ve ever been able to do.

LET'S WRAP THIS UP! Prey tell, what about voice over you ask? Well, in my search to figure what kind of actor I am I always had this little peep in the back of my head that told me, “ You’d really enjoy being a voice over actor for animation...just saying.” That peep turned into a yell as soon as I took my first voice over class. I stepped into that recording booth and that chubby cheeked cherub hit me in my soft spot and then I knew it. I felt home, like I belonged in that booth. Alas, this is the kind of actor I am! So I worked hard to educate and immerse myself in the voice over world and I must say I love it. Don’t get me wrong- I still enjoy performing on stage and working on films but there’s just something about voice over that just rocks my world.
